A Brief Look at Modern Gold Mines

Kerr-Sulphurets-Mitchell (KSM) Mine

The gold industry is still significant in the economy of British Columbia, for instance, it has “one of the largest undeveloped gold projects in the world” (Seabridge Gold, n.d.). This mine has a life of 55 years (estimated) and produces on average, 508,000 oz gold, with active exploration still ongoing in the year 2015. This is the Kerr-Sulphurets-Mitchell or KSM mine, owned and operated by Seabridge Gold Inc. and is proclaimed the largest gold project in Canada (Seabridge Gold, n.d.). Overall the mine has an estimated proven and probable 38.8 million ounces of gold and 10.2 billion pounds of copper (Seabridge Gold, n.d.).

New Afton Mine

Another modern mine in British Columbia is the New Afton Mine just west of Kamloops, it is owned entirely by New Gold, and it has been operating since 2012. This mine has an annual expected average production of 85,000 ounces of gold over its lifespan. The production of 2019 saw only 69,000 ounces while the 2020 production year saw an even lower 64,000 ounces (New Gold, n.d.).

Brucejack Mine

A third modern mine in British Columbia is the Brucejack mine, which has been operating since 2017 and has a mine life of 13 years, with total reserves of 4.2 million ounces of gold, 1,142,344 of which have been mined since production began. It is roughly 65 km north of Stewart and in the first three quarters of 2020 it had produced 259,443 ounces of gold and is owned by Pretivm (Pretivm, n.d.).
